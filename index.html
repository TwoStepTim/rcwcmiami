Message from ED

Engagement refers to the active involvement and participation of individuals, communities, and organizations in child welfare initiatives. It encompasses various aspects, including families, caregivers, educators, case managers, child welfare professionals, and community members, all working together to ensure the safety and well-being of children within our system of care. Engagement makes a difference through empowering families, strengthening relationships, preventing recurrence and fostering community support.

As we embark on this journey together that leads to better outcomes for our children and families, my hope is that you gain the knowledge and tools that better equip you through this conference to enhance your productivity for the community we serve. Continued collaborative efforts strengthens the safety net around our children and provides resources to allow our families thrive in their greatest capacities. Thank you always for your dedication and commitment to child welfare. ENGAGEMENT MAKES A DIFFERENCE!

- Bryttany Stringer-Hollman, Executive Director

Mission

To engage the community in advocating for a family-centered, culturally competent and outcomes-driven system of care that enhances the safety, permanency, and well-being of children and families.

We encourage our community to remain vigilant as we collaborate to support and foster change towards systemic equality. We choose to lead with love and unity in an effort to continue to better serve our community and families.

Mickael Charles

For over 20 years, Mickael Charles has worked with underserved youths in the Miami-Dade County community through providing life skills. Having grown up and worked in the same communities as the youths with whom he works provides Mickael with a unique understanding of the youths and their needs. Throughout his career, Mickael worked in several capacities including teaching, curriculum development and project management. Currently, Mickael is teaching life skills such as employment, money management, self-care, and activities for daily living to youths ages 13-23.

Domonique Rosario

Dominique Rosario works as the alumni coordinator with local non-profit Casa Valentina and is the secretary with Citrus Family Care Network’s Youth Advisory Council. She is a proud 2020 University of South Florida Graduate and enjoys frequent visits to her alma mater to enjoy the atmosphere. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What time does the conference begin?

Conference begins at 9:00a. Registration begins at 8:00a.

Will there be breakfast and/or lunch?

Light refreshments will be served for breakfast. Box lunch will be provided to all registered participants.

Is there specific parking for the event?

Yes, you can find parking information on the event details tab. Please remember to register your vehicle before entering venue. Please visit parking help desk with Allison Schild inside venue if you have any further questions.

How will I receive my certificate of participation?

All participants will receive their certificates via email post conference. Feedback form must be complete in order to receive certificate. In order to ensure you will receive your certificate, you must check-in with registration desk and complete feedback form.

When will I receive my certificate of participation?

Please allow 30 days post conference to begin distribution of certificates. If you have not received your certificate after 60 days, please contact Bryttany Stringer at

When will I receive CLE credits?

Please allow 30 days post conference to begin distribution of CLE credits. You must sign the CLE log at registration in order to receive credits. If you have not received your credits after 60 days, please contact Bryttany Stringer at Bryttany.Stringercbc@gmail.com

When will I receive CEU credits?

Please allow 30 days post conference to receive CEU credits. You must register with Jewish Community Services CEU table during registration in order to receive CEUs.

Do I still need to purchase a ticket if I am only attending the Awards Ceremony?

Yes, you must still purchase a ticket if you plan to attend any part of the Regional Conference.
